The main Computer Science programs offered for undergraduates are:

  1. Computer Science
  2. Software Design / Game Development
  3. Computing & Mathematics
  4. Biomedical Computing
  5. Cognitive Science
  6. Computing & Creative Arts
  7. Computing Major
  8. Science General/Minor
  9. Arts General/Minor

Queen's has great academic reputation. But that's about it. Their campus is in pretty bad condition with old building almost falling apart. The teachers aren't the best, almost irreproachable. It's like if they're looking at the students as if they're numbers. That said, there are some of them who are great teachers. The students are very sociable. They need to work on their maintenance more to become a perfect University.

To give you an idea:

Biomedical Computing
"Combines a strong Computing and Math component, foundational courses in the life sciences (Biology, Biochemistry,
and Microbiology), and specialized courses in Computational Biology, Computer-Integrated Surgery, and Medical
Informatics (105 core units plus 15 elective units)."

Courses you'll encounter:

  • Introductory Biology of Cells
  • General Chemistry
  • Introduction to Computing Science

Game Development
"According to the Entertainment Software Association of Canada, the 348 companies that make up the game
industry in Canada employ over 16000 people, and have $1.7 billion direct economic impact. Growth over the
last two years has been 11% and projected growth over the next two years is 17%. Leading companies in
Canada include Electronic Arts, Ubisoft, Activision Blizzard, Microsoft Canada, Nintendo of Canada, Sony
Computer Entertainment, and Disney Interactive Studios. According to industry spokesmen, intense labour
competition is making experienced staff harder to find, and (when found) to retain, and that the
post-secondary education system is not producing graduates in sufficient numbers or with skills that meet
industry expectations."

Courses you'll encounter:

  • General Physics
  • Game Design
  • Scientific Computing
  • etc.

Software Design
"S variant that focuses on software design and development; it has been accredited by CIPS according to
their more stringent Software Engineering criteria as well as their Computer Science criteria. There are
now two options within SODE: a general Software Development option and a specialized option in Game Development."

Courses you'll encounter:

  • Introduction to Computing Science
  • Linear Algebra
  • Logic for Computing Science
  • etc.
